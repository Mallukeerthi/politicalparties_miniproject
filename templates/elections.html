Indian Electoral System

Overview

India is the world's largest democracy, with over 900 million eligible voters. The electoral system is based on universal adult suffrage, where every citizen above 18 years of age has the right to vote.

Types of Elections

General Elections

Held every five years to elect members of the Lok Sabha (Lower House of Parliament). The party or coalition with a majority forms the government.

State Assembly Elections

Conducted to elect members of the State Legislative Assemblies. Each state has its own election schedule.

Local Body Elections

Include elections for Municipal Corporations, Municipal Councils, and Panchayats (village councils).

Electoral Process

Voter Registration

Citizens must register with the Election Commission to get their Voter ID card.

Nomination

Candidates file their nominations with the Returning Officer.

Campaigning

Political parties and candidates campaign for votes within the specified period.

Polling

Voters cast their votes using Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs).

Counting

Votes are counted and results are declared by the Election Commission.

Electoral Reforms

Recent Changes

Introduction of Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs), Voter Verified Paper Audit Trail (VVPAT), and online voter registration have modernized the electoral process.

Model Code of Conduct

A set of guidelines issued by the Election Commission to ensure free and fair elections. It regulates the conduct of political parties and candidates during elections.

Election Commission

Role and Responsibilities

The Election Commission of India is an autonomous constitutional authority responsible for administering election processes. It ensures free and fair elections, implements the Model Code of Conduct, and oversees the entire electoral process.
