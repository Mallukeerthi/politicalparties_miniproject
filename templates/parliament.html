Indian Parliament

Overview

The Parliament of India is the supreme legislative body of the Republic of India. It is a bicameral legislature composed of the President of India and two houses: the Rajya Sabha (Council of States) and the Lok Sabha (House of the People).

Rajya Sabha (Council of States)

Composition

The Rajya Sabha has a maximum of 250 members, of which 238 are elected by the state and union territory legislatures, and 12 are nominated by the President of India. Members serve six-year terms, with one-third of the members retiring every two years.

Lok Sabha (House of the People)

Composition

The Lok Sabha has a maximum of 552 members, of which 530 represent states, 20 represent union territories, and 2 are nominated by the President from the Anglo-Indian community. Members are directly elected by the people of India through general elections held every five years.

Powers and Functions

Legislative Powers

The Parliament has the power to make laws on subjects listed in the Union List and Concurrent List of the Constitution. It can also make laws on subjects in the State List under certain circumstances.

Financial Powers

The Parliament controls the finances of the government. It approves the annual budget and can levy taxes. The Lok Sabha has more powers in financial matters than the Rajya Sabha.

Executive Powers

The Parliament exercises control over the executive through various mechanisms like question hour, zero hour, and no-confidence motions. The Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to the Lok Sabha.

Parliamentary Procedures

Law Making Process

A bill must pass through several stages in both houses before it becomes a law. These include introduction, consideration in standing committees, discussion and voting in both houses, and finally, the President's assent.

Special Powers

The Parliament has the power to impeach the President, remove judges of the Supreme Court and High Courts, and amend the Constitution. It can also create new states or alter the boundaries of existing states.
